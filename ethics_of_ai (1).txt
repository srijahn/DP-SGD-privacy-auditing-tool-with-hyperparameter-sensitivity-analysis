Srijahnavi Chinthalapudi Deepanjali Kumari Chinthala Shivamani
Selected Paper: NeurIPS 2020 Paper
1 Problem Statement
Differential Privacy (DP) is widely used to protect sensitive data in machine learning models. One of the
most common techniques for training private models is Differentially Private Stochastic Gradient Descent
(DP-SGD).
DP-SGD uses several hyperparameters such as:
•Noise multiplier (σ)
•Clipping norm (C)
•Batch size
•Learning rate
•Number of training iterations
The trade-off between privacy and model accuracy is managed by these parameters.
Nevertheless, the real privacy leakage in practice is not always reflected in the theoretical privacy guar-
antee (ϵ).
The chosen paper suggests an auditing technique using poisoning attacks and demonstrates how actual
privacy leakage can deviate from theoretical bounds. Additionally, it notes that hyperparameters have a
significant impact on privacy but does not thoroughly examine that impact.
Thus, the goal of this project is to use empirical auditing attacks to investigate how various DP-SGD
hyperparameters impact actual privacy leakage.
2 Relation to Existing Work
Previous research presents an auditing technique that uses poisoning attacks to estimate a lower bound
onϵ. It demonstrates how noise, clipping norm, and randomness affect privacy.
Nevertheless, evaluation is constrained in terms of datasets and hyperparameter coverage. The work is
expanded by this project by:
•Systematically varying hyperparameters
•Testing multiple configurations
•Comparing theoretical and empiricalϵ

3 Methodology
Step 1: DP-SGD T rainingUse TensorFlow Privacy or Opacus to train models (logistic regression /
small neural networks) on datasets such as MNIST and Fashion-MNIST.
Step 2: Privacy AuditingTrain on clean datasetD 0 and poisoned datasetD 1. Distinguish outputs
to estimate empiricalϵusing auditing.
Step 3: Hyperparameter Study:Test different values of:
•Noise multiplierσ
•Clipping normC
•Batch size
•Learning rate
•Number of epochs
For each setting, compute theoreticalϵand measure empiricalϵ.
Step 4: AnalysisCompare theoretical vs empirical privacy and plot relationships such asϵvs noise,
clipping norm, and batch size.
4 Expected Outcomes
•Different hyperparameters produce different levels of privacy leakage
•Theoretical privacy guarantees may not match real-world privacy
•Some parameters affect privacy more than others
•Lower noise may increase leakage
•Smaller clipping norm may improve privacy
5 Contribution
•Extending the auditing method to analyze hyperparameter effects
•Providing systematic experimental evaluation
•Comparing theoretical and empirical privacy
•Identifying the most sensitive hyperparameters affecting privacy
6 Tools
Python, PyTorch/TensorFlow, Opacus/TensorFlow Privacy, NumPy, Matplotlib
7 Conclusion
This project studies the gap between theoretical and real privacy in DP-SGD and provides insights for
safer hyperparameter selection in privacy-preserving ML.
